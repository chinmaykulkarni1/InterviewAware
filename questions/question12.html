Calculating Power in a Resistor

In a complex circuit, there are four resistors: R1 = 10 ohms, R2 = 20 ohms, R3 = 30 ohms, and R4 = 40 ohms. R1 and R2 are connected in series, and this combination is connected in parallel with R3. This entire network is then connected in series with R4. If the voltage across the entire circuit is 120 volts, approximately what is the power dissipated by R3?

12 ohms

18 ohms

24 ohms

36 ohms

Correct ✔
Incorrect ✘
Answers
  • First, calculate the equivalent resistance of R1 and R2
  • Next, calculate the equivalent resistance of the parallel combination of R12 and R3
  • Now, add the resistance of R4 in series with the parallel combination
  • Find the voltage across the parallel combination of R12 and R3
  • Finally, calculate the power dissipated by R3 using the formula P = V^2/R

Designing a Voltage Follower Circuit

You are designing a circuit that requires a precision voltage reference. You decide to use an operational amplifier in the unity gain configuration as a voltage follower to buffer the voltage reference. The voltage reference produces an output of 2.5 volts, and you want to ensure that the output impedance of your voltage follower is as low as possible to minimize loading effects on the voltage reference.

Answers

  • The output impedance Z_out of the voltage follower circuit can be approximated using the formula:
  • Z_out = (1/A_ol)Z_in, where A_ol is the open loop gain, and Z_in is the input impedance
  • Substitute the values into the formula above. Z_out is approximately 1 ohm
  • This calculation assumes ideal behavior of the operational amplifier, which may not always hold true in practical circuits due to factors like finite bandwidth, slew rate limitations, and non-idealities in the op amp's internal circuitry.
  • Care must be taken to ensure the stability of the voltage follower circuit, especially when driving capacitive loads. Compensating for any phase shifts introduced by the operational amplifier can help maintain stability and prevent oscillations in the circuit.
